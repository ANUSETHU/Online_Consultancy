About BITSAT 2021

BITSAT 2021 Application Form has been released from 23rd February 2021. Admission will be offered into various Integrated First Degree Programmes. It is a university level entrance exam through which candidates will get admission into B.E, B.Pharma. & M.Sc. Programmes. The admission will be offered in the BITS campuses located in Pilani, Goa, & Hyderabad. The examination will be held by Birla Institute of Technology & Science. Here we are providing details such as exam dates, application form, its fee, and much more regarding BITSAT 2021.

BITSAT 2021 Application Form

Candidates must go through following details regarding BITSAT Application Form:

Candidates can submit the application form via online mode. BITSAT 2021 application form has been made available from 23rd February 2021. The last date to submit application form will be till 29th May 2021. Exam conducting authority will also provide correction facility from 27th to 31st May 2021. While filling application form, candidates must also upload passport size photograph & signature in the prescribed format. Candidates must also provide valid/ working email ID & phone number in the application form for further use. International applicants will also have to follow same application procedure to register for BITSAT. At last, candidates must also take printout of filled in application form for further use.

BITSAT Eligibility Criteria

Candidates are advised to go through following eligibility norms before applying for BITSAT 2021 examination:

For B.Pharma:

Qualification: Candidates must qualify 12th or equivalent examination from any recognized board or university.

Percentage: Candidates must score minimum 75% marks in all the subjects & 60% in each subject.

Mandatory subject: Candidates must have Physics, Chemistry, & Biology/ Mathematics with adequate proficiency in English in the qualifying examination.

For First degree program except B.Pharma:

Qualification: Candidates must qualify intermediate exam from any recognized board or university to be eligible for the examination.

Percentage: He/ She must score at least 75% marks in all the subjects & 60% in each subject.

Mandatory subject: Candidates must have Physics, Chemistry, & Mathematics with adequate proficiency in English.

Appearing: Appearing candidates will be also eligible for the examination.

BITSAT Exam Pattern

Candidates must go through following details regarding BITSAT Exam Pattern:

Exam Mode: BITSAT 2021 examination will be organized via online (computer-based) mode.

No. of questions: There will be total 150 questions in the examination.

Question Type: The Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ) will be asked in the examination.

Medium: Question paper will be in English language medium.

Parts: The question paper will consist of 4 parts.

Time Duration: Candidates will get 3 hours to complete the examination.

Marking scheme: Each correct answer will consist of 3 marks.

Negative marking: 1 mark will get deduct for each incorrect answer.

The section wise distribution of questions will be as mentioned below:

Section Subject No. of Questions
I Physics 40
II Chemistry 40
English Proficiencyand & Logical Reasoning 15 and 10
III Biology/ Mathematics 45
- Total 150

BITSAT 2021 Syllabus

The BITSAT examination will consist of NCERT class 11th & 12th syllabus. BITSAT syllabus will consist of topics as Physics, Chemistry, Biology/ Mathematics, Logical Reasoning, and English Proficiency. Some of the topics under all subjects will be as:

Physics: Topics under Physics will be as units & measurement, rotational motion, mechanics of solid, Heat & thermodynamics, rotational motion, etc.

Chemistry: The various topics will be as atomic structure, states of matter, physical & chemical equilibrium, s & p-block elements, principles of organic chemistry, hydrocarbons, and much more.

Mathematics: Some of the topics will be as algebra, trigonometry, three-dimensional geometry, differential calculus, linear programming, etc.

English Proficiency: Vocabulary, Grammar, Reading Comprehension, Analogy, and Composition

Logical Reasoning: Verbal reasoning- Analogy, Classification, reading passage, and many others. Non-verbal reasoning- Figure Matrix, paper cutting, rule detection, and many other topics.
